# Dracula2 - Exfiltration Progression Pitch

## Short Pitch

A gothic exfiltration game where you play Dracula during the final nights before the Demeter sails. Hunt, enthrall, sabotage, and prepare your passage while the surrounding villages awaken to what you are.

## Core Reframe

The game is not about Dracula surviving frightened villagers. It is about Dracula completing a strategic exfiltration before the land closes around him.

Dracula is powerful, old, arrogant, and dangerous. The villagers are not individually stronger than him. They matter because they can act during daylight, organize faster than one person can be everywhere, bless roads, burn refuges, destroy earth-boxes, delay cargo, and force him into bad timing.

The real enemy is not the mob. The real enemy is exposure, consecration, daylight, logistics, and the shrinking map.

## Main Objective

Prepare the Demeter voyage before dawn on the final night.

To leave Transylvania, Dracula must:

- prepare and move native-earth crates toward the port
- secure papers, coin, manifests, or a shipping arrangement
- open a route from castle to port
- protect or relocate daylight refuges
- misdirect, weaken, or divide the villagers
- enthrall useful human agents such as a clerk, coachman, sailor, innkeeper, or priest's assistant
- board or seal the cargo before the villagers understand the full plan

The countdown is not "hide for seven days." The countdown is "complete the passage before the countryside becomes impossible to cross."

## Night Loop

Each night, Dracula chooses a limited number of actions before dawn.

Possible night actions:

- Feed to regain strength, reduce hunger, or create leverage over a household.
- Scout the village, road, monastery, port, or catacombs.
- Move an earth crate one step closer to the road, coach depot, river, or port.
- Enthrall a human agent who can act during the day.
- Sabotage bells, watchfires, roads, bridges, kennels, or church defenses.
- Recover old castle assets such as wolf kennels, mist rites, sealed crypt passages, or buried coin.
- Silence a witness, with the risk of increasing fear and investigation.
- Forge or steal travel documents, manifests, cargo papers, or seals.
- Prepare the Demeter cargo.

The player should feel predatory at night. Dracula is not creeping around because he is weak. He is choosing where the countryside will bleed, lie, sleep, or fail tomorrow.

## Day Loop

During the day, Dracula cannot freely correct mistakes. The villagers, church, officials, and enthralled agents act according to the previous night's state.

Possible day responses:

- villagers search the castle grounds
- a road is watched or blocked
- a bridge is guarded or burned
- a graveyard or crypt is blessed
- an earth crate is discovered or destroyed
- a suspected thrall is questioned
- port cargo is delayed or inspected
- church bells or relics are moved into position
- one safe route becomes unsafe
- one refuge becomes known
- fear rises, causing mistakes, panic, or harsher countermeasures

Day is where the strategic pressure lands. Dracula is not "waiting scared"; he is bound by mythic law. His night choices decide how much damage the day can do.

## Progression Structure

### Phase 1: The Castle Still Holds

The first nights teach the player that the castle is a power base, not a prison.

Goals:

- establish the first earth crates
- learn the day/night rhythm
- identify the road and port route
- feed without creating too much alarm
- acquire the first human agent

Tone:

Dracula feels in control. The villagers are distant noise, rumor, and smoke below the mountain.

### Phase 2: The Villages Wake

The countryside begins to connect incidents.

Goals:

- move crates beyond the castle
- divide the villages before they coordinate
- secure a coach or cart route
- prevent the church from sanctifying key roads
- gain a day-acting agent in the port or customs chain

Tone:

The danger becomes logistical. Villagers are still prey at night, but they are becoming a system by day.

### Phase 3: Consecrated Ground

The map starts to close.

Goals:

- keep at least one route to the port viable
- protect remaining earth crates
- counter relics, bells, patrols, and blessed thresholds
- choose whether to be subtle or brutal
- decide which assets can be abandoned

Tone:

Dracula's power is still terrifying, but careless power now creates heat. The player should feel the arrogance of the monster meeting the arithmetic of daylight.

### Phase 4: The Demeter Window

The ship is reachable only for a short window.

Goals:

- move the final crate or seal the cargo
- keep the shipping papers valid
- prevent last-night inspection
- board, hide, or transfer through an agent
- leave enough confusion behind that the departure is not stopped

Tone:

This is the exfiltration climax: not a boss fight, but a tightening net of choices, routes, and consequences.

## Player Fantasy

The player fantasy is not "Dracula the victim."

It is:

- Dracula as a predator with an itinerary
- Dracula as a nobleman using fear, law, money, blood, and superstition as tools
- Dracula as a supernatural strategist constrained by old rules
- Dracula as cargo, corpse, aristocrat, rumor, wolf, mist, and plague depending on the hour

At night, he is the monster.

By day, he is a secret that must not be found.

## Villager Threat Model

Villagers should rarely be dangerous because a single villager can beat Dracula. They are dangerous because they can:

- see what he cannot see while he sleeps
- act where he cannot act during the day
- destroy the infrastructure of escape
- create blessed no-go zones
- change the social state of the map
- force him to spend night actions repairing damage instead of advancing the plan

The mob is a pressure system, not a boss monster.

## Quest Language

Avoid framing tasks as timid survival goals.

Weak framing:

- Survive until the Demeter leaves.
- Avoid the angry villagers.
- Do not get caught.

Stronger framing:

- Prepare the passage.
- Move the native earth.
- Secure the manifest.
- Silence the bell tower.
- Turn the clerk.
- Keep the road unblessed.
- Divide the villages.
- Seal the cargo before dawn.

This keeps Dracula active and dangerous.

## Trailer Shape

The trailer should sell contrast:

1. A castle room, still and luxurious.
2. Dracula crosses a blood-red threshold at night.
3. A villager window goes dark.
4. An earth crate is dragged through mist.
5. A church bell begins ringing at dawn.
6. Daylight map state: roads marked, fires lit, one refuge burned.
7. Night again: Dracula chooses another route.
8. The port and the Demeter appear as the objective.
9. Final line: "Prepare the passage before the land wakes."

## Design North Star

Every system should preserve one idea:

Dracula is not afraid of villagers. Dracula is afraid of time, sunlight, consecration, and exposure.

The game is compelling when the player feels powerful at night, constrained by day, and responsible for turning raw horror into a successful escape.
